Personal defense guide

Simple habits that stop common attacks.

Use this checklist before logging in, paying online, downloading files, or connecting to unknown networks.

URL

Inspect the address

Read the full domain from right to left. Attackers rely on lookalike letters, extra words, and fake urgency.

MFA

Protect every key account

Use unique passwords, a password manager, and multi-factor authentication for email, banking, cloud, and social accounts.

OTP

Never share one-time codes

Support teams should not ask for OTPs, recovery codes, or full passwords through chat, calls, or email.

FILE

Treat attachments carefully

Unexpected invoices, resumes, compressed files, and executable files should be verified before opening.

WIFI

Be cautious on public Wi-Fi

Avoid sensitive work on unknown networks. Keep sharing off and prefer trusted networks or mobile data.

UPD

Update devices quickly

Security updates close known weaknesses. Turn on automatic updates for your operating system, browser, and apps.

If something feels wrong

Pause, disconnect, report, reset.
